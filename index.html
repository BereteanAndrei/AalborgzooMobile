You can make a difference

Since its opening in 1935, Aalborg Zoo puts great importance to the conservation and education of endangered species and sustainability. Through the Aalborg Zoo Conservation Foundation (AZCF) we take part in various global projects on animal conservation, breeding, education, research and focus on fair trade.

The Black Mamba is AZCF’s biggest and most successful project, a unique nature conservation and anti-shoot shooting program.

The Black Mamba Project

What do we do?

The Black Mamba anti-poaching unit’s main objective is the security of the reserve and the protection of wildlife.

26 brave women create shelters for elephants and other types of wildlife, each of them patrols the area for around 20km/day and they arrest and scare off poachers, while destroying snares and traps.

They want their communities to understand that the benefits are greater through wildlife conservation rather than poaching, addressing the social and moral decay.

Our other projects

Since our establishment, we have been supporting multiple projects that benefit nature and wildlife in Denmark and the rest of the world.

The Elephants project

Improves elephants’ home by protecting them from illegal hunting and capturing.

The Payamin project

Supports the Indians in Payaminola City with their struggle in training and conservation of the rain forests.

Zoos Go Wild

Aims at encouraging zoos in Europe to collaborate on nature conservation projects.
